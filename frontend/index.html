Key Features

Comprehensive tools and technologies designed to enhance coastal safety through community participation and advanced analytics.

Crowdsourced Reporting

Submit geotagged reports with photos and videos of ocean hazards directly from your mobile device with GPS precision and offline capabilities.

Social Media Analytics

AI-powered monitoring of Twitter, Reddit, and YouTube discussions to understand ground realities and track information spread patterns.

Real-time Dashboard

Interactive map visualization with dynamic hotspots, live data streams, and comprehensive analytics for authorities and citizens.

Multilingual Support

Available in Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Malayalam, and other 15+ regional languages to ensure widespread community participation across India.

About SAGE

Social Analytics and Geo Intelligence Engine - Revolutionizing coastal safety through community-driven intelligence and advanced technology. India boasts one of the world's longest coastlines at 11,098.81 km, encompassing 13 states and union territories. Surrounded by the Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and Indian Ocean, our coastline supports over 250 million people while facing diverse oceanic challenges from tsunamis to cyclones.

Our Mission

SAGE empowers coastal communities across India to actively participate in maritime safety through crowdsourced reporting and real-time social media intelligence. We bridge the gap between citizen observations and official response systems to create a comprehensive hazard monitoring network.

Our Vision

To establish India as a global leader in community-driven ocean hazard management, where every coastal citizen becomes a guardian of maritime safety through technology-enabled reporting and collaborative intelligence systems.

How It Works

SAGE combines geotagged citizen reports with AI-powered social media monitoring to create real-time hazard maps. Our platform processes multilingual content, validates reports through community verification, and provides predictive analytics to forecast potential danger zones.
